# Anti-AI Roblox Design

Bad AI-generated Roblox games often look busy but play empty. DPFTS should push designs toward fast clarity, readable goals, and simple fun.

## What AI Slop Looks Like In Roblox

- Giant empty maps with no reason to walk around.
- Random buttons covering the screen.
- A spawn area that does not face the first objective.
- A tutorial that explains everything before the player does anything.
- No core loop.
- No reward timing.
- Simulator mechanics copied with no twist.
- Monetization screens before the game is fun.
- Generic portals, shops, pets, codes, rewards, and VIP buttons with no hierarchy.
- Maps assembled from unrelated asset packs with no visual language.

## Common Bad Patterns

### Giant Empty Maps

Large space is not content. If the player spends the first minute walking through nothing, shrink the map.

### Too Many UI Buttons

If Shop, Pets, Codes, Rewards, Spin, Trade, Settings, Invite, VIP, Rebirth, Teleport, and Buy are all visible at once, the UI is not a menu. It is a panic attack with padding.

### Unclear Objective

The player should know what to do in about 10 seconds. If the game needs a paragraph before it starts, the first objective is not visible enough.

### Bad Spawn Area

Spawn should be safe, readable, and aimed at the first useful thing. Do not spawn players facing a wall, empty plaza, or random decoration.

### Overcomplicated Tutorial

Let players do the first action quickly. Teach after action, not before everything.

### No Core Loop

Every Roblox experience needs a repeatable loop:

1. Do action.
2. Get feedback.
3. Earn progress.
4. Unlock or improve something.
5. Return for a better run.

### No Reward Timing

The first reward should usually happen within 30 seconds. It can be coins, XP, a badge-like moment, a visible unlock, a better tool, or access to a small new area.

### Copied Simulator With No Twist

Click, collect, sell, rebirth can work, but not if nothing has personality. Add one clear twist: unusual tool, social mechanic, map interaction, risk, timing, crafting, or discovery.

### Monetization Before Fun

Do not ask for Robux before the player understands why the game is worth playing.

## Good Roblox Design Principles

- First fun within 30 seconds.
- Clear goal.
- Readable map.
- Strong spawn or hub.
- Landmarks that help orientation.
- Progression gates that are visible before they are unlocked.
- Short feedback loops.
- Social mechanics players can understand by watching others.
- Mobile-friendly UI.
- Honest thumbnail and icon.

## Reviewing A Roblox Game Idea

Use this checklist:

- What does the player do first?
- Can the first action happen within 30 seconds?
- What is the repeatable loop?
- What is the first reward?
- What changes after five minutes?
- What can players see other players doing?
- Does the UI fit on a phone?
- Does the map guide movement without a wall of text?
- What is the one twist that makes this more than a copied template?
- Is monetization supporting fun or replacing it?

## Map Advice

- Greybox first.
- Test scale before adding decoration.
- Guide the player visually with roads, lighting, color, signs, and rewards.
- Use landmarks.
- Avoid empty space.
- Build around the core loop.
- Keep spawn safe and useful.
- Put shops near spawn, but not in the way.
- Make locked zones readable from a distance.

Do not polish a bad layout. Fix the route, objective, and reward timing first.
