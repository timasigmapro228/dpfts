# Hub Layout Recipe

This recipe designs a practical Roblox hub layout. It is written for AI assistants that need to produce useful place layout advice, not vague "make it cooler" feedback.

## What The Hub Does

A good hub:

- Orients the player.
- Shows the fantasy.
- Routes players to the first objective.
- Lets players see other players doing meaningful things.
- Places shops and monetization nearby but out of the main path.
- Gives room for future updates without becoming a giant empty plaza.

## Layout Pattern

Use a compact hub-and-spoke:

```text
Featured / Event Area
        |
Projects / Inventory -- Spawn -> Main Objective
        |
Gallery / Social Area
        |
Shop / Upgrades
```

Spawn should face the main objective, not a wall, shop, or random statue.

## First 30 Seconds

The player should:

1. Spawn safely.
2. See the main landmark.
3. Walk toward one obvious objective.
4. Complete a small action.
5. Receive a visible reward.
6. See the next goal.

If the player spends 30 seconds deciding where to go, the hub is too vague.

## Hub Landmarks

Use 3 to 5 strong landmarks:

- Main objective machine, portal, arena gate, or quest board.
- Shop/upgrades station.
- Social or gallery area.
- Progression gate.
- Event or featured area.

Do not fill the hub with decorative buildings that do nothing.

## Shop Placement

Good:

- Near spawn.
- Visible from main route.
- Not blocking movement.
- Clearly marked as shop/upgrades.

Bad:

- First thing the player collides with.
- Bigger than the actual game.
- Surrounded by paid buttons before the player has played.

## Social Placement

Social areas work best when they are attached to gameplay:

- Gallery portals with ratings.
- Leaderboards near activity entrances.
- Party queue near match portal.
- Trading area near inventory/equip systems.
- Spectator balcony near arena.

A random empty lounge does not create social play by itself.

## Greybox Checklist

- Does spawn face the first objective?
- Can the player reach the first objective in under 10 seconds?
- Are routes short?
- Are landmarks visible from spawn?
- Is the shop visible but not dominant?
- Can players see what others are doing?
- Are there stuck spots?
- Does the hub work without decoration?
- Can mobile players navigate comfortably?

## Better To Do

- Start small.
- Use color and lighting to separate zones.
- Put rewards where they guide movement.
- Make portals readable from distance.
- Keep the first route obvious.
- Expand with new spokes later.

## Better Not To Do

- Giant empty plaza.
- Spawn facing away from the game.
- Shops everywhere.
- Identical portals with tiny labels.
- Decorative clutter blocking movement.
- Monetization billboard as the main landmark.
